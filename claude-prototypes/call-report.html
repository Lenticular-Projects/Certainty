Executive Summary

This call is a Missed Opportunity with a Certainty Score of 28/100. Mrs. Massey called as a textbook Money Caller with a specific, stated financial need: a spending card covering utilities — the light bill, water bill, phone bill. She said it at 7:06 with zero ambiguity. She was GREEN for the entire call. There was no resistance, no fear, no stalling. She came ready to enroll into the right plan and Robert never found a way to make what was available feel like a win.

The call collapsed at 13:18 when Robert confirmed the best available plan was a $30 quarterly OTC card — less than she expected — and accepted that outcome without a single pivot. He never annualized the $30 to $120/year. He never connected it to the bills she specifically mentioned. He never reframed the $30 against the $0 she was currently getting. He surrendered with "best of luck to you" — and she was polite enough not to argue. A completed Three-Step Math Breakdown using Mrs. Massey's own words would have given this call a legitimate chance. It was never attempted.

Pattern 2 — Incomplete Math Breakdown Never executed — closest: 13:00
What happened
Mrs. Massey told Robert the number in her head: "$1,900 for paying the light bills." Robert responded by explaining that the Part B giveback wasn't available and the best OTC was $30 quarterly. He never annualized $30 to $120/year. He never reframed against the $0 Humana was providing. He never connected either number to the light bill, water bill, or phone bill she named at 7:06.

What the Math Breakdown should have sounded like at 13:00

Here's what I want to make sure is clear before we decide anything: right now, Humana is putting zero dollars toward those bills. Zero. The Aetna plan puts $120 a year back toward James — that goes toward exactly what you told me matters: the light bill, the water bill. It's not everything you were hoping for. But it's $120 more than what he's getting today. Do we want to at least get that working for him?"
