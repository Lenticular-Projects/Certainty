## Certainty Score Breakdown

### Lead Score: 14 / 20

Lawrence held a generally competent authority frame for the majority of the call — he moved through discovery efficiently, didn't hedge on the plan presentation, and reframed the zip code change as a positive without breaking stride. The deduction comes from two areas. First, the enrollment confirmation sequence at 19:58 — when Bruce said he didn't understand the benefits, Lawrence's response was to ask if he wanted it explained again rather than stating the re-explanation as a given: *"Let me walk you through it one more time."* That is a small but real permission ask at a moment that required a statement. A leading agent adapts delivery to the person in front of them.

> **To improve this score:** When the client signals confusion or fatigue, don't ask if they want clarification — provide it. State and move. "Let me make this simple for you" is leadership. "Did you want me to go over it again?" is a permission ask.
